Does the proposed hero improve journeys? Does it serve the site's four audiences? Honest assessment.

Current hero vs. proposed direction

Current

What improves

Problem clarity (3-second test). "Blind spots in critical rotating equipment" is instantly understood. The current "Not just the critical 10%" requires the reader to do maths. "Blind spots" is a problem every plant manager recognises without needing context.
Mechanism credibility (6-second test). Naming "Electrical Signature Analysis" gives the engineer something concrete. The current lead explains the problem at length (3 sentences before getting to the solution). The proposed version leads with the mechanism in sentence one.
Impact strip replaces proof bar. The current proof bar (10,000+ assets, 5,300+ findings, €80M+, 80+ customers) is all about Samotics. The proposed strip mixes what-you-get with scale signals: detection accuracy, ROI, install speed, fleet size. More useful to a buyer deciding whether to click deeper.
Subheadline is shorter. 2 sentences vs. 3. Gets to "no sensors on the machine" and "weeks to months of advance warning" faster.

What doesn't improve — or gets worse

The "fleet coverage" angle disappears. The current hero has a strong positioning claim: you're only monitoring 10% of your assets. That's a provocative number. The proposed version trades it for a safer "blind spots" frame. Both work, but "10%" creates urgency that "blind spots" doesn't. Consider keeping the 10% stat somewhere — maybe the value stack or the impact strip.
"Eliminate" is strong but risky. You can't eliminate all blind spots. A reliability engineer will think: "really, all of them?" Consider "Close the blind spots" or "See the assets your sensors can't reach" — still strong, but defensible.
The proof bar numbers are strong and shouldn't be lost. €80M+ detection value and 5,300+ validated findings are powerful scale signals. If you move to an impact strip, these either go into the strip or into the social proof section lower on the page. Don't delete them — relocate them.
"300–800% ROI in water utilities" in the impact strip is too narrow for a homepage. A metals buyer reads that and thinks: "this is a water product." Either generalise to "300–800% ROI across industrial deployments" or drop the vertical reference entirely. Save water-specific numbers for the water page.

Why this version over the V1 proposal

Headline "See the assets your sensors can't reach" — describes the outcome, not the category. "Eliminate blind spots" felt like a category label. This version makes you picture the specific assets you're missing. It's also the actual tagline thread running through every industry page ("where sensors can't go").
Lead Starts with what SAM4 does (detects faults), names the method (ESA), names the install point (MCC), gives the time (20 min), gives the outcome (weeks to months). Five facts in two sentences. No philosophy.
Impact strip Dropped "in water utilities" from the ROI claim. Now says "300–800% proven ROI" — defensible across verticals. Kept 10,000+ and 80+ as scale anchors. Added "90%+ detection accuracy" as the technical credibility signal.
What moved €80M detection value and 5,300+ findings move to the Social Proof section (section 5 on the homepage). They're scale stats, not hero stats. The hero should sell the outcome, not the company's track record.

Does this improve the site as a whole?

The thread that connects everything

Homepage: "See the assets your sensors can't reach"
→ Water: "Protect water infrastructure without the guesswork"
→ Energy: "Monitor the assets that keep energy flowing"
→ Chemicals: "Monitor equipment in ATEX zones — without entering them"
→ Metals: "Keep your mills running and your mines dewatered"
→ Airports: "Keep baggage handling systems running — every flight, every day"
→ Pulp & Paper: "Detect faults before they stop the paper machine"

The homepage hero now sets up the industry pages. "See the assets your sensors can't reach" is the universal promise. Each industry page answers: "here's what that means for your specific assets." The current hero ("Monitor your entire fleet") is about coverage volume. The proposed hero is about coverage access — which is what the industry pages actually argue.
One inconsistency to fix: The Energy Production hero ("Monitor the assets that keep energy flowing") is the weakest link. It doesn't continue the "can't reach" thread. Consider tightening it to something like "Detect faults in the assets your sensors can't reach — offshore, underground, and in ATEX zones" to maintain the thread. This was flagged in the buyer journey audit too.
The footer tagline already says it: "Monitoring the assets your sensors can't reach." The proposed hero headline aligns the top and bottom of every page. Right now the hero and the footer are saying different things.
