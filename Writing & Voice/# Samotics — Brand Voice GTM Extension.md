\# Samotics — Brand Voice GTM Extension  
\*\*Version 1.0 | March 2026\*\*

This document turns the Samotics Brand Voice into market-specific GTM execution.  
Use it to write web pages, sales sequences, decks, case studies, demos, and partner collateral.

\---

\#\# 0\) Global GTM rules (apply everywhere)

\#\#\# The Samotics voice never changes  
Calm. Precise. Proof-first. Direct.  
We adjust \*\*depth and emphasis\*\*, not personality.

\#\#\# Proof requirements (by funnel stage)  
\- \*\*Awareness:\*\* 1 concrete stat OR 1 concrete asset example  
\- \*\*Consideration:\*\* 1 quantified outcome \+ method summary (MCC/ESA)  
\- \*\*Validation:\*\* 2–3 quantified proof points \+ constraints \+ references  
\- \*\*Purchase:\*\* TCO / rollout plan \+ security posture \+ implementation reality  
\- \*\*Expansion:\*\* fleet economics \+ program governance \+ internal case study pack

\#\#\# Market meta-drivers to keep present  
\- \*\*Motor systems dominate electricity in industry\*\* — position energy as a first-class value stream.  \[oai\_citation:0‡IEA 4E\](https://www.iea-4e.org/emsa/publications/policy-brief-electric-motor-systems-why-are-they-important/?utm\_source=chatgpt.com)

\- \*\*Regulation \+ enforcement is tightening in water\*\* — speak to pollution risk with restraint and evidence.  \[oai\_citation:2‡GOV.UK\](https://www.gov.uk/government/news/new-financial-penalties-for-environmental-offences?utm\_source=chatgpt.com)

\#\# 1\) Core markets and the GTM voice mapping

\#\#\# Market A — Water & Wastewater Utilities (UK \+ Europe)  
\*\*Why they buy\*\*  
\- Pollution risk and enforcement pressure  
\- Remote estates and inspection burden  
\- Energy reduction targets  
\- Visibility on submersible pumps and pumping stations

\*\*External pressure points to reference (carefully, not sensationally)\*\*  
\- UK water regulators are imposing large penalties for wastewater failures.  \[oai\_citation:3‡Ofwat\](https://www.ofwat.gov.uk/thames-yorkshire-and-northumbrian-water-face-168-million-penalty-following-sewage-investigation/?utm\_source=chatgpt.com)    
\- UK government has proposed faster penalties for environmental offences up to £500,000.  \[oai\_citation:4‡GOV.UK\](https://www.gov.uk/government/news/new-financial-penalties-for-environmental-offences?utm\_source=chatgpt.com)  

\*\*Voice emphasis\*\*  
\- Lead with \*\*pollution prevention \+ operational confidence\*\*  
\- Then \*\*inspection reduction \+ fleet coverage\*\*  
\- Then \*\*energy efficiency as measurable savings\*\*

\*\*Primary hero\*\*  
\- Maintenance and reliability lead with an estate problem (hundreds–thousands of sites)

\*\*Primary objection\*\*  
\- “We already do inspections / vibration / telemetry”    
Response pattern: blind spot → cabinet-based monitoring → clogging/failure modes → proof → rollout.

\*\*Language anchors\*\*  
\- Use: “pumping stations”, “submersible pumps”, “blockage / clogging”, “pollution incident prevention”, “remote sites”  
\- Avoid: “digital transformation”, “smart water”, “seamless”

\*\*Top-of-funnel headline patterns\*\*  
\- “Detect pump blockages before they cause pollution events.”  
\- “Monitor submersible pumps without site visits.”

\*\*Mid-funnel proof blocks\*\*  
\- “Detect developing faults weeks to months in advance.”  
\- “Install from the MCC. No sensors on the asset.”

\*\*Validation assets\*\*  
\- Case study format: incident risk → detection → action → avoided fine/cleanup \+ avoided downtime  
\- Include constraints transparently (RPM thresholds, calibration period)

\*\*CTA\*\*  
\- “Talk to an engineer about your pumping stations.”  
\- “Review a real blockage detection example.”

\#\#\# Market B — Chemicals & Materials (incl. ATEX environments)  
\*\*Why they buy\*\*  
\- Hazardous areas complicate sensor installation and maintenance  
\- High consequence downtime  
\- Reliability programs cover only a fraction of the motor fleet

\*\*External pressure points\*\*  
\- Hazardous-area monitoring requires ATEX / IECEx compliance and creates practical deployment constraints.  \[oai\_citation:5‡The AEMT\](https://www.theaemt.com/resource/navigating-condition-monitoring-challenges-in-hazardous-areas.html?utm\_source=chatgpt.com)    
\- Downtime economics can reach very high ranges in industry surveys; keep claims sourced and framed as “industry estimates.”  \[oai\_citation:6‡ABB Group\](https://new.abb.com/news/detail/129763/industrial-downtime-costs-up-to-500000-per-hour-and-can-happen-every-week?utm\_source=chatgpt.com)  

\*\*Voice emphasis\*\*  
\- Lead with \*\*reach \+ safety practicality\*\*  
\- Then \*\*coverage between maintenance rounds\*\*  
\- Then \*\*energy waste detection\*\*

\*\*Primary hero\*\*  
\- Reliability engineer \+ maintenance manager managing ATEX constraints

\*\*Primary objection\*\*  
\- “We cannot add sensors in Zone 1/2”    
Response: cabinet-based measurement reduces field hardware → compliance conversation shifts to cabinet/installation → fewer field failure points.

\*\*Language anchors\*\*  
\- Use: “hazardous areas”, “ATEX zones”, “cabinet-based monitoring”, “reduced field instrumentation”  
\- Avoid: “wireless mesh”, “deploy anywhere” (too absolute)

\*\*Content structure that works\*\*  
\- Problem: “Your vibration program covers what you can reach.”  
\- Constraint: ATEX \+ access \+ uptime  
\- Shift: MCC-based ESA  
\- Proof: early faults \+ avoided event \+ rollout speed

\*\*CTA\*\*  
\- “Assess your ATEX monitoring coverage.”  
\- “Review an MCC-based deployment plan.”

\#\# 2\) Buying-stage playbooks (by market)

\#\#\# Stage 1 — Awareness (category creation)  
\*\*Goal:\*\* name the blind spot \+ make SAM4 concrete.

\*\*Formula\*\*  
\- “Most \[asset class\] goes unmonitored because \[constraint\].”  
\- “SAM4 monitors from the MCC using ESA.”

\*\*Proof minimum\*\*  
\- 1 number OR 1 concrete example.

\*\*Recommended content types\*\*  
\- Category pages  
\- “ESA vs vibration” explainer  
\- 90-second “inside the MCC” visuals

\---

\#\#\# Stage 2 — Consideration (solution fit)  
\*\*Goal:\*\* show mechanism \+ outcomes \+ where it wins.

\*\*Formula\*\*  
\- Mechanism in one paragraph  
\- 3 outcomes (uptime, cost, energy)  
\- Complementarity with vibration

\*\*Proof minimum\*\*  
\- 1 named outcome \+ 1 constraint stated honestly

\*\*Recommended content types\*\*  
\- Product page  
\- How-it-works page  
\- Industry landing page

\---

\#\#\# Stage 3 — Validation (risk removal)  
\*\*Goal:\*\* kill fear: accuracy, false alarms, security, rollout.

\*\*Required modules\*\*  
\- Accuracy and false alarm framing  
\- Calibration expectations  
\- Security posture (OT-friendly)  
\- Integration reality (CMMS/API, phased)

\*\*Security language anchors\*\*  
\- Use: “OT environments”, “segmentation”, “wired communication”, “risk-based”  
\- If mentioning standards, do it carefully and factually: ISA/IEC 62443 is a recognised industrial cybersecurity framework.  \[oai\_citation:14‡Dragos\](https://www.dragos.com/blog/isa-iec-62443-concepts?utm\_source=chatgpt.com)  

\*\*Recommended content types\*\*  
\- Technical validation pack (PDF)  
\- Reference call sheet  
\- Deployment plan one-pager

\#\# 3\) Channel-specific tone knobs

\#\#\# Website  
Highest discipline. Short sentences. Scan-first.  
Every section needs one concrete anchor.

\#\#\# Sales sequences  
More direct. More situational empathy.  
Less explanation, more “here’s what happens in plants like yours.”

\#\#\# Decks  
Use “Problem → Shift → Proof → Plan” structure.  
Numbers on slides. Plain language in speaker notes.

\#\#\# Case studies  
Restrained. Evidence-based.  
Show detection → action → impact.  
Include limitations when relevant.

\#\# 4\) Voice enforcement rules for humans \+ AI

\#\#\# Ban list (global)  
\- revolutionary, game-changing, best-in-class, world-leading  
\- seamless, cutting-edge, next-generation  
\- delve, navigate, landscape, harness, embark, journey

\#\#\# “If you can’t quantify it, don’t claim it”  
No “nearly / virtually / mostly” on performance.

\#\#\# The scan test  
If headings alone don’t tell the story, rewrite headings.

\#\#\# The engineer test  
If a maintenance engineer wouldn’t say it out loud, it doesn’t belong.
