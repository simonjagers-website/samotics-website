## 1. Core Value Proposition

### Primary statement

**Every AC motor already tells you when something is wrong — through its electrical signature. Samotics listens.**

SAM4 uses AI-driven Electrical Signature Analysis (ESA) to monitor the health, performance, and energy efficiency of rotating equipment — 24/7, from the motor control cabinet. No sensors on the machine. No production downtime to install. Detect over 90% of developing faults weeks to months before they cause unplanned downtime.

### Why this matters (the problem)

Electric motors drive 70% of industrial electricity consumption. Yet most rotating equipment goes unmonitored between scheduled maintenance rounds — or isn't monitored at all because it's submerged, enclosed, or in a hazardous zone. The result: unplanned failures, wasted energy, pollution events, and maintenance teams stuck in reactive mode.

### What SAM4 changes (the shift)

SAM4 installs at the electrical cabinet — not on the machine — and captures high-frequency current and voltage data that reveals the condition of the entire drivetrain: motor, transmission, coupling, and driven load. AI models trained on thousands of industrial assets detect anomalies in their earliest stages, turning every connected motor into a continuous condition monitoring sensor.

## 5. Category Positioning

### Category definition

Samotics operates in **AI-driven Electrical Signature Analysis (ESA)** — a distinct and emerging category within condition monitoring and predictive maintenance.

ESA traces its origins to 1985, when Oak Ridge National Laboratory developed Motor Current Signature Analysis for nuclear power plant monitoring. Samotics has advanced this into a commercial, AI-powered platform that monitors the full drivetrain — motor, transmission, coupling, and driven load — using only current and voltage data captured at the MCC.

### How Samotics positions against the category

| Dimension | Traditional vibration monitoring | Samotics SAM4 (ESA-based) |
|---|---|---|
| **Sensor location** | Mounted on the asset | Installed at the MCC |
| **Access requirement** | Physical access to the machine | No access to the machine needed |
| **Installation time** | Hours to days per asset (often requires shutdown) | <30 minutes per asset, no shutdown |
| **Monitoring mode** | Periodic (walk-around) or continuous (expensive) | Continuous, 24/7 |
| **Hard-to-reach assets** | Difficult or impossible | Full coverage — submerged, enclosed, hazardous, remote |
| **Fault coverage** | Primarily mechanical | Electrical + mechanical + process deviations |
| **Drivetrain coverage** | Motor and immediate surroundings | Full drivetrain: motor → transmission → coupling → load |
| **Energy insights** | Not typically included | Built-in: efficiency monitoring, rightsizing, waste identification |
| **Cost per point** | High (sensor + wiring + installation) | Lower (clamp-on current measurement from MCC) |
| **AI/ML layer** | Varies, often manual analysis | AI-driven anomaly detection trained on thousands of assets |

### Known objections and recommended responses

| Objection | How to address |
|---|---|
| **"We already have vibration monitoring"** | SAM4 isn't a replacement — it's the layer that covers what vibration can't. Submerged assets, enclosed drives, ATEX zones, remote sites. And it adds continuous monitoring between your periodic rounds. Many customers run both. |
| **"How accurate is it really? False positives?"** | SAM4 detects >90% of developing faults. The AI model improves as it learns your specific assets. Expect a calibration period of weeks — after which detection quality is high. We'd rather alert you early than miss something. |
| **"The learning phase takes too long"** | Set clear expectations: the model needs operating data to calibrate. Value builds over weeks, not days. Your CSM works with you through the learning phase to validate early detections. |
| **"It's not plug-and-play"** | ESA is a different monitoring approach than vibration. It requires context — understanding your assets, your processes, your maintenance triggers. That's why every customer gets a dedicated CSM, not just a login. |
| **"Can it detect what vibration detects?"** | ESA detects both electrical and mechanical faults across the full drivetrain. For some mechanical issues (e.g. specific bearing faults on driven equipment), vibration may offer higher resolution. The two approaches are complementary — ESA adds reach, continuity, and electrical fault coverage that vibration doesn't have. |
| **"Too expensive at scale"** | Frame the cost per avoided failure, not per monitoring point. One detected bearing failure at ArcelorMittal saved €50K+. One pollution prevention at Yorkshire Water avoided €150K in fines. Average payback is 8 months. |
| **"Language and localisation"** | Platform and support available in 6 languages (EN, DE, NL, FR, IT, ES). Continuing to expand. Some customers note the importance of native-language support — we're investing here. |
| **"Data access and integration with our CMMS"** | Acknowledged as a growth area. Roadmap includes deeper CMMS integration and more open data access. Raise this early so we can plan together. |
