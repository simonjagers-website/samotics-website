Product use case

Preventing failure in wastewater inlet screws

Asset Inlet screw pump
Fault detected Belt, gearbox & bearing degradation
Industry Water & Wastewater
Advance warning Up to 5 months

The situation

What was at stake

Inlet screws are the front line of wastewater treatment. When one fails, raw sewage backs up — triggering pollution events, regulatory fines, and emergency mitigation that dwarfs the repair cost. The top three failure modes are belt degradation, gearbox wear, and motor bearing damage. Traditional vibration sensors and manual inspections often miss these faults until the belt is near rupture or the gearbox is grinding.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4 monitors current and voltage at the motor control cabinet — not on the screw itself. Its algorithms detect rising energy at belt-related frequencies, gearbox fault signatures, and bearing cage indicators up to five months before failure. When a fault develops, the system sends a notification with fault type, severity, and a specific maintenance recommendation. Yorkshire Water trialled SAM4 on 23 wastewater assets and is now rolling it out to 4,500 assets across clean water and wastewater networks.

The outcome

What happened next

Customers schedule belt retensioning or replacement at a convenient time — no emergency callouts, no pollution events, no production shutdowns. One Yorkshire Water deployment paid for the entire project within a month of installation, twenty times over. The shift from reactive to predictive maintenance frees resources for root-cause analysis and operational optimisation.

“Within a month of installation, SAM4 Health saved us enough on inlet screw repairs and mitigation to pay for the entire project, twenty times over.”

Simon Rhodes Business Transformation Manager, Yorkshire Water
