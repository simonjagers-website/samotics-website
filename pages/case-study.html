The challenge

70,000 annual inspections. Most of them told them nothing.

Yorkshire Water's condition monitoring programme relied on periodic vibration surveys and manual inspections. The economics didn't work: most assets were submersible pumps — physically inaccessible without pulling them from the ground. Vibration sensors couldn't be installed. Inspections were scheduled by calendar, not by condition.

The result: 30% of their rotating assets had no condition monitoring at all. Failures at unmanned stations went undetected until alarms triggered or sewage overflowed. Each pollution event carried regulatory, financial, and reputational consequences — costs that dwarfed the repair bill.

The solution

SAM4 deployed at the MCC — no access to the pump required

Samotics installed SAM4 ESA monitoring across Yorkshire Water's pump network. Current and voltage sensors were clamped at the motor control cabinet — not on the asset. No site modifications. No confined space permits. No disruption to operations.

Fleet-scale rollout

1,300 assets across 600+ sites connected in phases. Each installation took minutes at the MCC — no pump extraction, no scaffolding, no production downtime.

Continuous monitoring

Every connected pump monitored 24/7. SAM4 detects bearing degradation, blockages, cavitation, and efficiency losses as they develop — weeks before failure.

Integrated with operations

SAM4 alerts integrated into Yorkshire Water's existing CMMS. Maintenance teams receive actionable alerts with fault context — no interpretation required.
