Fault detection

Yorkshire Water prevents pollution event with automated clogging detection

Asset Sewage pumps (co-located pair)
Fault detected Complete blockage in both pumps
Industry Water & Wastewater
Result Pollution prevented

The situation

What was at stake

Two co-located sewage pumps at a Yorkshire Water pumping station had telemetry systems in place for visibility. One day in September, the telemetry indicated one pump had tripped while the other was still running normally. Since the remaining pump could handle demand temporarily, no immediate intervention was scheduled. But the telemetry was wrong.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4's automated clogging detection feature signalled that both pumps had actually become completely blocked — a situation that could cause both to fail and trigger a pollution event. SAM4 sent an urgent alert to Yorkshire Water. The maintenance team immediately visited the site and confirmed both pumps were clogged. The telemetry system had generated a false signal. When the team lifted the pumps, they found boots, tyres, and other foreign objects causing the blockages.

The outcome

What happened next

Both pumps were cleared and returned to normal operation — confirmed by the SAM4 dashboard. Without SAM4's alert, the site would have relied on faulty telemetry and the blockages would have gone undetected until overflow. This was the only visibility the team had: the telemetry had failed, and SAM4 was the sole early warning system that worked.

“Temporary issues with the site telemetry meant the only visibility we had was via the Samotics unit. Thanks to the alert system we were able to urgently attend the site, investigate and remove blockages that would have led to pollution of the local environment.”

Simon Rhodes Business Transformation Manager, Yorkshire Water
