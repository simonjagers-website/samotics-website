What SAM4 measures

Continuous energy intelligence, asset by asset

SAM4 uses Electrical Signature Analysis to measure energy performance from the motor control cabinet. No additional sensors. No energy sub-meters. Every motor monitored continuously.

BEP deviation monitoring

SAM4 measures how far each pump or fan operates from its best efficiency point. Continuous tracking shows whether efficiency is stable, improving, or degrading — and by how much.

Oversized motor detection

Motors running consistently at partial load waste energy. SAM4 identifies oversized motors across the fleet and quantifies the savings from rightsizing — without a dedicated energy audit.

kWh savings attribution

Every efficiency improvement is tracked and attributed to a specific asset and action. Plant managers get reportable energy outcomes, not estimates — sourced from continuous measurement.

Degradation-to-waste linkage

A bearing fault doesn't just risk a breakdown — it wastes energy. SAM4 connects mechanical degradation to energy impact, so maintenance decisions factor in both reliability and cost.

ESG-ready data

Measured outcomes your sustainability team can report

SAM4 energy data is auditable, exportable, and attributable to specific assets. Sustainability teams get measured kWh savings — not modelled estimates — for ESG reports, regulatory filings, and board presentations.

Measured, not modelled

SAM4 calculates energy savings from continuous electrical signature data — baseline vs current consumption per asset. Auditors prefer measured outcomes over engineering estimates.

CO₂ equivalent calculations

kWh savings translate directly to CO₂ reductions using your grid emission factor. SAM4 quantifies the environmental impact alongside the financial impact.

Exportable data

Energy performance data is exportable as CSV or via API. Integrate with your existing ESG reporting tools, or use SAM4's built-in reporting for standalone submissions.
