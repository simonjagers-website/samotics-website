Fault detection

Three faults fixed in a 30 kW oil transfer pump before complete failure

Asset Oil transfer pump (30 kW)
Fault detected Worn coupling spider, stuck vanes, loose foundation
Industry Industrial manufacturing
Faults found 3

The situation

What was at stake

A 30 kW oil transfer pump was degrading across multiple components simultaneously — a worn jaw coupling spider, stuck pump vanes, and a completely loose foundation that left the pump hanging against the piping for support. Any one of these issues could eventually cause failure. Together, they made total breakdown a matter of time.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4 flagged an increase at multiple harmonics of the pump's rotational frequency, combined with an elevated noise floor. Samotics notified the customer and placed the pump on orange alert. One month later, the customer inspected and found all three degraded components. The spider in the jaw coupling was worn out, the vanes were stuck, and the foundation had come completely loose. They repaired all three problems, and the pump returned to normal operation — confirmed by SAM4.

The outcome

What happened next

A single SAM4 alert led to the discovery and repair of three simultaneous faults. This detection was one of a dozen that built the customer's confidence in the system. They are now moving from pilot-phase scepticism to planning inspections based on SAM4 alerts.

“This result is one of a dozen that have helped our engineers trust that when SAM4 says something’s wrong, something really is wrong. We’re starting to move from ‘let’s see if it works’ to actually planning our inspections based on SAM4’s alerts.”
