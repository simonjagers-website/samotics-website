What SAM4 monitors

Every rotating asset in your network

SAM4 uses Electrical Signature Analysis to monitor motor-driven assets from the MCC. No sensors on the asset. No access to wet wells. No confined space entry.

Submersible pumps

The hardest assets to monitor with traditional methods. Submerged, inaccessible, and typically unmonitored. SAM4 reads the electrical signature from the MCC — detecting blockages, impeller damage, and bearing degradation without going near the wet well.

Booster pumps

Critical for pressure management across the distribution network. SAM4 monitors efficiency and mechanical health continuously — surfacing degradation before pressure drops or energy costs spike.

Borehole pumps

Deep-well pumps that can't be inspected without extraction. SAM4 detects pump wear, motor degradation, and efficiency loss from the surface — avoiding costly pull-and-inspect cycles.

Blowers & aerators

The largest energy consumers in wastewater treatment. SAM4 monitors bearing health and efficiency — catching degradation early and identifying units operating below their best efficiency point.

Circulation pumps

Process-critical for treatment works. SAM4 provides continuous health monitoring without adding sensors to chemically aggressive or hard-to-reach locations.

Regulatory & energy

Monitoring that serves compliance and efficiency

SAM4 data doesn't just prevent failures. It generates evidence for OFWAT performance commitments, supports pollution prevention reporting, and identifies energy savings across the pump fleet.

OFWAT performance commitments

Continuous monitoring data provides auditable evidence of asset health management. SAM4 logs every alert, every diagnostic, and every recommended action — creating a compliance trail that manual inspections cannot match.

Pollution prevention

Every pump failure at a wastewater pumping station is a potential pollution event. SAM4 detects degradation weeks before failure — giving maintenance teams time to act before sewage reaches a watercourse.

Energy efficiency

Pumping accounts for up to 80% of a water utility's electricity consumption. SAM4 identifies pumps operating below their best efficiency point — enabling targeted optimisation without capital expenditure. Yorkshire Water targets a 28% electricity reduction using SAM4 data.

AMP planning evidence

Fleet-wide health data supports asset management planning with real condition data, not age-based assumptions. Prioritise capital investment where it's needed — backed by continuous monitoring evidence.
