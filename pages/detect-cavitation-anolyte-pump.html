45 kW anolyte pump saved from complete breakdown

Asset Horizontal centrifugal pump (45 kW)
Fault detected Cavitation & mechanical seal damage
Industry Chemicals
Savings €15,000

The situation

What was at stake

A 45 kW horizontal centrifugal pump circulates anolyte in an electrochemical production process. Cavitation — the formation and collapse of vapour bubbles inside the pump — was gradually weakening the mechanical seal. Left unchecked, the seal would fail completely, destroying the pump and requiring a full replacement.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4 detected an increase in the current spectrum noise floor around the rotational frequency, combined with a drop in active power — clear indicators of cavitation. When the system flagged sustained cavitation, Samotics alerted the customer to the likely presence of collateral damage. The customer inspected the pump and found that the mechanical seal was beginning to leak.

The outcome

What happened next

The customer conducted a minor seal repair instead of replacing a badly damaged pump. They reported this saved them €15,000 in avoided replacement costs. Early detection turned what would have been a catastrophic failure into a planned, low-cost maintenance action.
