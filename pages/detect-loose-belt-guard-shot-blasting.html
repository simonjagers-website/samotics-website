Fault detection

Loose belt guard caught early in shot blasting machine

Asset Shot blasting machine (blast wheels)
Fault detected Loose belt guard
Industry Surface treatment manufacturing
Detection to maintenance 5 weeks

The situation

What was at stake

A four-wheel shot blasting machine operates in a surface treatment facility. Each blast wheel is belt-driven. When a belt guard loosens, it does not just affect the wheel it belongs to — the vibration disturbs neighbouring wheels as well. Standard maintenance would not have flagged this issue until the next scheduled inspection.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4 flagged a sudden spike at the rotational frequency for two of the four blast wheels. Over the following days, intensity continued to rise for both — while the other two wheels remained normal. Five weeks later, during a planned maintenance stop, the customer inspected both flagged wheels. One had a loose belt guard; the other showed no visible problem. The customer tightened the belt and replaced the damaged guard on the faulty wheel, after which both returned to normal operation.

The outcome

What happened next

SAM4 identified a fault that affected two machines but originated in one — a diagnostic insight that manual inspection would likely have missed. The maintenance team used the alert to expand their inspection scope during the planned stop, catching an issue they had not originally scheduled for attention.

“SAM4 kept us from overlooking parts that needed attention but weren’t originally scheduled for inspection during this stop.”
