What SAM4 detects

Seven fault types. One sensor location.

All detections come from current and voltage signatures captured at the motor control cabinet. No sensors on the pump. No wiring to the asset. No site visits for installation.

Bearing degradation

Inner race, outer race, and rolling element defects detected through characteristic fault frequencies in the electrical signature. Typical lead time: 4–12 weeks before failure.

Full detection

Cavitation

Vapour bubble collapse at the impeller creates broadband noise in the electrical signature. SAM4 detects cavitation onset before it causes impeller erosion or seal damage.

Full detection

Blockage

Rag, debris, and sediment buildup detected through load signature changes. Critical for wastewater sewage pumps where blockages cause overflows and pollution events.

Full detection

Imbalance

Impeller erosion, deposit buildup, or broken vanes create rotational imbalance visible in the current spectrum. Detected at 1× running speed.

Full detection

Seal wear

Mechanical seal degradation changes the load profile. SAM4 flags progressive seal wear before it causes leakage, contamination, or motor ingress.

Full detection

Misalignment

Shaft or coupling misalignment between motor and pump shows as 2× running speed components. Causes accelerated bearing and seal wear if left uncorrected.

Full detection

Efficiency loss

Power consumption vs. output deviation reveals pumps operating below best efficiency point. SAM4 quantifies the gap and estimates energy savings potential.

How it works for pumps

From cabinet to diagnosis in three steps

01

Sensor installs at the MCC

A single SAM4 sensor clips onto the motor supply cables inside the motor control cabinet. No wiring to the pump, no confined space entry, no process interruption. Installation takes less than 30 minutes per asset.

02

ESA captures the full drivetrain

Current and voltage waveforms are sampled continuously. The electrical signature carries information about every component in the drivetrain — motor, coupling, gearbox, and the pump itself. Faults anywhere in the chain are visible.

03

AI + engineers validate every alert

AI models detect anomalies and classify fault types. A Samotics reliability engineer reviews every alert before it reaches your team — with a specific diagnosis, severity rating, and recommended action. No false positives. No noise.
