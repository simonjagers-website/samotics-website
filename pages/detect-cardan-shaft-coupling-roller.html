Failing cardan shaft coupling detected in runout table roller

Asset Runout table roller
Fault detected Failing cardan shaft coupling
Industry Steel manufacturing
Detection accuracy 100%

The situation

What was at stake

In a hot strip mill, runout table rollers cool steel slabs as they move down the line. The cardan shaft couplings connecting motors to rollers are subject to extreme fatigue from the thermal and mechanical stress. When a coupling fails without warning, the roller goes offline — delaying the entire line and risking damage to the strip.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4 flagged a sudden spike in the first current interharmonic at all the roller's operating points. Several events with the same signature had been previously diagnosed on this and other rollers at this customer's facility, so Samotics was confident in the diagnosis: a fatigued, loosening coupling. The customer was notified and the asset placed on red alert. One week later the customer replaced the coupling, and the roller's electrical signature returned to normal.

The outcome

What happened next

SAM4 has caught every coupling failure in advance on these rollers — and never flagged one that was not real. The customer treats Samotics notifications as a trusted source and acts accordingly. This detection also benefited from historical data: the same fault signature had been observed and confirmed on multiple occasions.
