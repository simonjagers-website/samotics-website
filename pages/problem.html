The cost of inaction

The cost isn't just the repair bill

Unplanned downtime

A single pump failure at a remote site costs €50,000+ in emergency repair, lost production, and overtime. Multiply that across a fleet of unmonitored assets, and the annual exposure runs into millions.

Pollution and regulatory fines

A sewage pump failure causes a spill. The fine alone can reach £390,000. Add the remediation cost, the regulatory investigation, and the reputational damage. One event resets years of compliance work.

Safety exposure

Every manual inspection in a confined space, ATEX zone, or submerged environment puts personnel at risk. The safest inspection is the one your team never has to make.

Wasted energy

A pump operating 7% below its best efficiency point wastes thousands in electricity per year. Across hundreds of unmonitored motors, the energy waste is invisible until someone measures it.

Why the gap exists

Three environments your programme can't reach

Condition monitoring requires proximity to the asset. Three barriers prevent it.

01

Physical access

Submersible pumps sit 20 metres underwater. Sealed blowers run inside enclosed housings. High-temperature motors operate in environments that destroy sensors within months. You can't mount what you can't reach.

Submerged pumps Enclosed drives High-temp motors
02

Economics

A vibration sensor costs $500. Installed on a submerged pump, that becomes $5,000+ after scaffolding, permits, shutdown, and specialist labour. At that price, most assets stay dark. The monitoring business case collapses before it starts.

$500 → $5,000+ installed Scaffolding and permits Production shutdowns
03

Regulation

ATEX zones require explosion-proof equipment and certified personnel for any installation. Confined space entry needs permits, standby teams, and safety protocols. The regulatory overhead makes routine sensor deployment impractical for hundreds of assets.

ATEX certification Confined space permits Hot work procedures
