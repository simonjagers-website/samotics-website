Fault detection

Process insights prevent long-term damage to 200 kW centrifugal pump

Asset Centrifugal product pump (200 kW, VFD-controlled)
Fault detected Cavitation from suboptimal startup procedure
Industry Chemical manufacturing
Root cause Operating procedure fixed

The situation

What was at stake

A 200 kW VFD-controlled centrifugal product pump was being ramped up to high speed at startup, even when the fluid level in the tank was very low. The mismatch between available NPSH and pump RPM induced severe cavitation — damaging the impeller, bearings, and mechanical seal with every occurrence.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4 flagged a sudden and significant rise in the noise floor around the pump's supply frequency — indicative of cavitation. Samotics alerted the customer, who inspected the pump and reviewed the records of that pumping session. They discovered the pump's speed had been rapidly ramped up while the tank level was too low for the positive displacement stripping pump to take over. The root cause was not a mechanical fault but a suboptimal startup procedure.

The outcome

What happened next

The customer changed the operating procedure to match the product pump's speed at startup to the tank's current condition. This eliminated a recurring source of impeller, bearing, and seal damage across this and other centrifugal product pumps. SAM4 did not just detect a fault — it provided the insight needed to identify and fix the underlying process issue.
