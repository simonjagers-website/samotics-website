Loose cardan joint caught the same day SAM4 was installed

Asset Runout table roller
Fault detected Loose cardan shaft coupling
Industry Steel manufacturing
Time to detection Same day

The situation

What was at stake

A hot strip mill customer installed SAM4 on a new runout table roller. The roll and its motor were identical to others SAM4 had been monitoring at the facility for two and a half years — giving the system a deep pool of baseline data in its library of failure signatures.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

When the first measurements came in, SAM4 immediately flagged the asset. The noise floor around the supply frequency and the roll's rotational frequency were both elevated — clear indicators of a loose coupling, based on confirmed detections in identical rolls. Normally SAM4 needs a four-week training phase for new assets, but the existing 2.5 years of data from similar rolls made instant diagnosis possible. Two days later the customer confirmed the cardan joint was loose and replaced it.

The outcome

What happened next

Same-day detection on a newly installed asset. The value of SAM4 increases over time as its library of failure signatures grows. The more assets it monitors and the longer it monitors them, the faster and more accurate its diagnoses become.
