What SAM4 monitors

Every motor-driven asset in your mill

From the stock preparation line to the paper machine, SAM4 monitors health and efficiency from the MCC. No sensors on the asset. No production disruption.

Refiners

The most energy-intensive equipment in a pulp mill. SAM4 monitors refiner motor health and energy efficiency — detecting plate wear, bearing degradation, and efficiency drops that indicate maintenance or optimisation opportunities.

Pumps

Stock pumps, process pumps, and water pumps throughout the mill. SAM4 detects cavitation, blockages, bearing wear, and BEP deviation — combining reliability and efficiency monitoring in a single view.

Fans & blowers

Dryer section fans, ventilation systems, and process air blowers. SAM4 monitors imbalance, bearing health, and energy consumption — catching degradation before it affects paper quality or energy costs.

Compressors

Air compressors and vacuum pumps supporting the paper machine. SAM4 detects valve faults, bearing issues, and efficiency losses — reducing both downtime risk and compressed air costs.

Energy intelligence

Quantified energy savings, asset by asset

SAM4 doesn't just flag efficiency problems — it quantifies them. Every asset has a measured energy baseline, a current consumption profile, and a calculated deviation. You know exactly where kWh are being wasted and what to do about it.

BEP deviation monitoring

SAM4 measures how far each pump or fan operates from its best efficiency point. Continuous tracking shows whether efficiency is stable, improving, or degrading — and by how much.

Oversized motor detection

Motors running consistently at partial load waste energy. SAM4 identifies oversized motors across the fleet and quantifies the savings from rightsizing — without a dedicated energy audit.

kWh savings attribution

Every efficiency improvement is tracked and attributed to a specific asset and action. Plant managers get reportable energy outcomes, not estimates — useful for ESG reporting and internal business cases.

ESG-ready reporting

Energy savings data is exportable and auditable. Sustainability teams get the numbers they need for ESG reports, regulatory filings, and board presentations — sourced from continuous measurement, not modelling.
