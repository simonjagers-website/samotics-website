The challenge

Downtime in baggage handling is measured in missed flights

Airport conveyor systems run thousands of hours per year under constant load. A single motor failure on a baggage line can delay flights, strand luggage, and damage passenger trust. Maintenance windows are narrow.

Zero-tolerance for disruption

Passengers expect bags to arrive. A conveyor breakdown at peak hour cascades into flight delays, lost bags, and reputational damage. SAM4 gives advance warning to prevent this.

Narrow maintenance windows

Baggage systems operate nearly around the clock. Planned maintenance must fit between flights. SAM4 identifies which assets need attention so you can prioritise the right repairs in limited time.

Scale and complexity

Large airports have hundreds of conveyor motors and drives spread across terminals. SAM4 monitors them all centrally from motor control cabinets — no sensors on the belts.

How it works

Simple installation, immediate insights

SAM4 installs inside the motor control cabinet — no sensors on the asset, no production interruption, no confined-space entry.

  1. Install in the MCC Clip-on current and voltage sensors attach to existing wiring in the motor control cabinet. Takes 20 minutes per asset.
  2. Connect to SAM4 cloud The edge device samples at 20 kHz and sends data securely to the SAM4 platform for AI analysis.
  3. Get actionable alerts Receive predictive alerts weeks to months before failure, with fault type, severity, and recommended action.
