The problem

Water assets fail silently — and expensively

Water and wastewater utilities operate thousands of pump sets across distributed networks. Most run unmonitored between scheduled maintenance rounds. The consequences of an undetected fault range from service interruption to environmental prosecution.

Sewage pump blockages

Blockages cause overflows and pollution incidents. By the time operators respond, the spill has already happened. SAM4 detects blockage presence, location, and severity in real time — before the overflow.

Submerged asset access

Pulling a borehole pump for inspection costs thousands and takes the asset offline for days. SAM4 reads the electrical signature from the MCC — no crane, no diver, no downtime.

Ageing infrastructure & regulatory risk

Degrading rotors, worn seals, and failing bearings across treatment works and pumping stations go unnoticed until they trigger environmental incidents and six-figure fines. Ofwat ties performance commitments to pollution prevention — unplanned failures create compliance exposure that reactive maintenance cannot manage.

How it installs

No sensors in the wet well

SAM4 installs inside the motor control cabinet — no sensors in the wet well, no confined-space entry, no disruption to pumping operations.

  1. Install in the MCC Clip-on current and voltage sensors attach to existing wiring in the motor control cabinet. Takes 20 minutes per asset.
  2. Connect to SAM4 cloud The edge device samples the electrical signal at 20 kHz and sends data securely to the SAM4 platform for AI-driven analysis.
  3. Get actionable alerts Receive predictive alerts weeks to months before failure, with fault type, severity, and recommended action.
