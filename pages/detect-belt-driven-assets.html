The situation

What was at stake

Chemical plants contain belt-driven pumps, conveyors, fans, and compressors that keep the production process running. Many of these assets sit in ATEX zones or harsh environments where workers cannot safely access them for routine inspection. A single belt failure can shut down an entire production line, contaminate product batches, or create safety hazards. Traditional monitoring requires sensors mounted on the asset — impractical in these conditions.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4 monitors from the motor control cabinet, making it safe and practical for ATEX zones. Across multiple chemical plant deployments, SAM4 detected: (1) belt degradation on a chlor-alkali circulation pump — flagged through rising energy at the belt frequency, confirmed at next planned stop; (2) bearing and pulley degradation on a Kevlar production exhaust fan — detected just three weeks after installation, preventing contamination of product batches; and (3) belt degradation on a salt conveyor — caught before the belt snapped and halted the production line.

The outcome

What happened next

Each detection was confirmed by maintenance and resolved during planned maintenance windows. No unplanned downtime, no product contamination, no safety incidents. The major advantage: SAM4 sensors install in the MCC, far from ATEX-zoned production systems, reducing the cost of equipment, installation, and cabling compared to traditional techniques.

“The major reason we chose SAM4 is because it’s easy to install in the motor control center, far away from our ATEX-zoned production systems. That greatly reduces the cost of equipment, installation and cabling, compared to traditional condition monitoring techniques.”

Joost Cuyckens Asset Management Leader, Kuraray
