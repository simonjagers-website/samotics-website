Debris clogging detected in wastewater collection pump

Asset Municipal wastewater collection pump
Fault detected Clogging causing cavitation
Industry Water & Wastewater
Result Timely action taken

The situation

What was at stake

A municipal wastewater collection pump was operating in a network where debris regularly enters the system. When debris clogs the pump intake, the pump starts cavitating — increasing wear on internal components and reducing flow capacity. If left undetected, the pump overheats and fails.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4 flagged a sudden increase in the noise floor around the pump's supply frequency, which continued over the following days. Based on earlier detections, Samotics contacted the customer to report sustained cavitation and suspected clogging. Ten days later, the customer removed debris from the pump's intake. The pump returned to normal operation, confirmed by the SAM4 dashboard.

The outcome

What happened next

The customer acted on the alert before the pump sustained permanent damage. This was one of many such detections SAM4 has made on this type of asset — each one giving the maintenance team enough advance warning to schedule a targeted intervention.
