What you get

Four components. One managed service.

SAM4 is not a single product. It's four components working together — each one purpose-built for industrial motor monitoring, delivered and managed by Samotics.

Sensor + comms hardware

Dedicated hardware installed at the MCC. Captures the electrical signature of every connected AC motor at up to 96 kHz and transmits data via cellular, with Ethernet or WiFi fallback where signal is limited. No sensors on the machine. No wiring to the asset.

Signal processing + AI analytics

Proprietary algorithms extract mechanical and electrical features from the signal. Machine learning models — trained on thousands of industrial assets — detect anomalies, classify fault types, and track degradation over time.

Monitoring service

Every AI-flagged anomaly is reviewed by a Samotics reliability engineer before an alert reaches your team. Fault type confirmed, severity assessed, recommended action written. No false positive noise. No alerts to triage. This is what makes SAM4 a managed service, not a self-serve tool.

Dashboard + API

A fleet-wide interface for daily use and quarterly reporting. Asset health scores, active alerts, diagnostic history, and trend analysis — all in one view. Your engineers see everything we see. Role-based access for different teams. REST API for CMMS integration and automated workflows.

Alert logic

Every alert is validated before it reaches your team

Reliability teams don't need more alerts. They need fewer, better ones. SAM4's three-stage pipeline ensures every alert that reaches your team has been validated by a reliability engineer.

1

AI detection

Machine learning models identify anomalies in the electrical signature. The models are trained on thousands of industrial assets across fault types and equipment categories.

2

Expert validation

Every anomaly flagged by the AI is reviewed by a Samotics diagnostician before an alert is sent. Human expertise confirms fault type, assesses severity, and writes a recommended action. No automated spam.

3

Alert delivery

Validated alerts arrive in the SAM4 dashboard and push to your CMMS, email, or notification channel. Each alert includes: fault type, severity level, affected asset, recommended action, and estimated timeline.

Severity levels

Critical

Immediate action required. Failure likely within days to weeks. The alert includes specific remediation steps.

Warning

Developing fault detected. Plan maintenance within the next scheduled window. Degradation trend visible but not yet urgent.

Advisory

Minor anomaly or process deviation. Monitor the trend. No immediate action, but the condition is now tracked.

Diagnostic depth

What your team receives with every alert

Every validated alert comes with a diagnostic report written by a Samotics reliability engineer. Fault type, severity, recommended action, and full historical context — ready to act on.

Fault type

Bearing degradation, misalignment, imbalance, cavitation, clogging, rotor bar defects, winding degradation, power supply issues, or process deviations. Classified by the AI, confirmed by the diagnostician.

Severity assessment

How far the fault has progressed. Baseline comparison, degradation rate, and estimated time to functional failure. Not just "something is wrong" — how wrong, and how fast it's getting worse.

Recommended action

Specific maintenance recommendation written by the diagnostician. "Inspect bearing at next scheduled shutdown" or "Replace coupling within 4 weeks." Actionable, not generic.

Historical context

Full timeline: when the anomaly first appeared, how it has evolved, and how the asset compares to similar equipment across your fleet. Pattern recognition across thousands of assets informs every diagnosis.
