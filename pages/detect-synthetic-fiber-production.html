Product use case

Preventing failure in synthetic fiber production

Asset Heated godet roll
Fault detected Motor overloading
Industry Synthetic fibre manufacturing
Savings per detection $90,000

The situation

What was at stake

Godet rolls are essential in synthetic fibre production — they heat-treat yarn at extremely high temperatures. If a roll fails, the entire production line shuts down for emergency repairs that can take hours. Traditional sensors melt or break in these conditions. Vibration sensors cannot work on slow-rotating rolls. And the assets cannot be approached by maintenance staff while running.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4 monitors from the motor control cabinet — far from the extreme temperatures. Its algorithms detected that the motor driving a heated roll was overloading: amperage levels exceeded the motor's rated current. The system immediately sent an alert, advising the customer to inspect the motor for signs of overheating and check insulation integrity. Within 30 minutes, the maintenance team inspected the motor and adjusted the tension on the roll. SAM4 confirmed the roll was operating healthily again.

The outcome

What happened next

The customer reported that $90,000 in downtime costs were avoided. If the roll had failed during service, it would have caused 12 hours of downtime: $70,000 in lost production plus $20,000 in corrective maintenance. The timely warning made it possible to inspect and adjust the asset without shutting down the line. Across 14 rolls on one production line, the estimated annual profit from SAM4 monitoring is $283,500.
