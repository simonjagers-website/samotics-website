The challenge

Hazardous zones make monitoring dangerous, expensive, or impossible

Rotating assets in ATEX zones, offshore platforms, and confined spaces are the hardest to monitor — and the most expensive to lose. Traditional condition monitoring requires physical access, hot work permits, and ATEX-certified instrumentation. Most operators accept the gap.

✗

ATEX certification required for every sensor, cable, and junction box installed in the hazardous zone

✗

Confined space entry permits, hot work permits, and gas testing before each inspection visit

✗

Personnel exposure to hazardous atmospheres, high-pressure equipment, and elevated work platforms

✗

Monitoring gaps between scheduled rounds — failures happen when nobody is there to detect them

The SAM4 approach

Install in the safe zone. Monitor the hazardous zone.

SAM4 connects at the Motor Control Centre — outside the hazardous area. It reads the electrical signature of every connected motor, detecting mechanical faults, process anomalies, and efficiency losses without ever entering the zone.

No ATEX certification needed

SAM4 hardware installs in the MCC room — a safe zone. No explosion-proof housings, no intrinsic safety barriers, no ATEX certification for the monitoring system itself.

No confined space entry

No personnel need to access the hazardous zone for monitoring. No gas testing, no buddy systems, no rescue teams on standby. The MCC provides full visibility.

No hot work permits

Installation requires no welding, drilling, or modification in the hazardous area. SAM4 connects to existing motor cables at the MCC — typically in under 30 minutes per asset.

Zero production disruption

No shutdown required. SAM4 installs on live circuits using non-invasive current and voltage sensors at the MCC. Assets stay running throughout installation.

HSE officers care about reducing personnel exposure to hazardous zones. SAM4 eliminates the need for anyone to enter these areas for condition monitoring — permanently.

What SAM4 monitors

Across upstream, midstream, and downstream operations

Any motor-driven asset connected to an MCC can be monitored. SAM4 detects bearing degradation, mechanical imbalance, misalignment, process anomalies, and efficiency losses — from the electrical signature alone.

Compressors

Gas compressors in ATEX zones and offshore platforms. SAM4 detects bearing wear, valve faults, and efficiency degradation — without accessing the compressor skid.

Pumps

Injection pumps, transfer pumps, and process pumps across production, refining, and distribution. Continuous health monitoring from the MCC, including cavitation and blockage detection.

Fans & blowers

Ventilation fans, cooling fans, and process blowers. SAM4 detects imbalance, bearing degradation, and belt issues — critical for maintaining safe atmospheric conditions in enclosed spaces.

Generators

Backup and primary generators on offshore platforms and remote facilities. SAM4 monitors motor and driven-equipment health continuously — essential where failure means loss of power and evacuation.
