Fault detection

Borehole pump clogging detected nearly one year in advance

Asset Submerged borehole pump
Fault detected Iron buildup causing clogging
Industry Water & Wastewater
Advance warning ~1 year

The situation

What was at stake

A submerged borehole pump operated in an iron-containing well as part of a water utility's clean water supply. Over time, iron deposits accumulated at the pump's inlet — gradually restricting flow and causing the pump to work harder. Because borehole pumps are submerged deep underground, inspection requires lifting the pump to the surface: a costly, time-consuming operation.

What SAM4 detected

The signal and the diagnosis

SAM4 detected patterns of decreasing active power paired with multiple instances of cavitation — clear indicators of progressive clogging. The system flagged the iron deposit nearly a year before it would have caused failure. Samotics sent a notification as soon as the fault started developing. The customer decided to lift the pump from the well and conduct maintenance.

The outcome

What happened next

Following maintenance, SAM4 confirmed the pump's return to healthy behaviour. Without the early warning, the clogging would have led to overheating and a significant loss of water supply capacity. The detection was especially valuable given the pump's submerged nature — there was no other practical way to know the inlet was clogging.
