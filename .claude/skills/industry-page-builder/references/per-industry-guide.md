## 1. Water & Wastewater

**URL:** `/industries/water/`
**Status:** Live. Template page — all other industry pages follow its patterns.

### Hero
- Headline: "Your critical pumps sit where sensors cannot go"
- Constraint: Submerged/underground pumps inaccessible to physical sensors
- KPIs: GBP 20M+ documented benefits, 5,000+ pumps monitored, 0.32% false communication rate
- Measurement method: "Stats based on 1,874 resolved incidents across pumps, fans, and conveyors. January 2024 to February 2026."

### Trust bar
- Logos: Yorkshire Water, Northern Ireland Water, Vitens, Northumbrian Water, Southern Water
- Status: 5 named customers. Strong.

### Challenges (section 3)
1. Sewage pump blockages — overflows, pollution incidents, regulatory clock
2. Submerged asset access — pull costs thousands, takes days offline
3. Ageing infrastructure — degrading rotors, worn seals, six-figure Ofwat fines
4. Energy waste — oversized pumps and degraded impellers waste 10-35% of energy

### Why SAM4 (section 4)
1. No sensors on the asset — monitors from the MCC, no wet well entry
2. Works across full fleet — pumps, blowers, agitators, mixers (1 kW to large aeration blowers)
3. Detects what vibration cannot — blockages, cavitation, seal wear, efficiency loss
4. Deploys without downtime — clip-on CTs, no shutdown, no confined-space entry
- Contrast line: "Traditional vibration sensors require physical access to the asset. For submerged, remote, or hazardous pump sets, that access does not exist."

### Assets (section 5)
1. Pumps — submersible, borehole, booster, sewage, process
2. Blowers & fans — aeration blowers, ventilation fans
3. Agitators & mixers — oxidation ditch rotors, mixers, scrapers

### Proof (section 6)
- Featured: Yorkshire Water — 70,000 inspection trips replaced, 1,000+ stations, GBP 748K losses prevented
- Card 1: Southern Water — GBP 150K potential fines avoided, seal degradation detection
- Card 2: NI Water — 1 bolt remaining, coupling bolt detection on sewage pump
- Card 3: Vitens — 7.1% pump station efficiency improvement

### Installation (section 7)
- Headline: "No sensors in the wet well"
- Quote: Simon Herrington, Yorkshire Water: "Pollution prevention through early failure mode detection on sewage pumping assets."

## 7. Adding a new industry

When building a page for a new industry vertical:

1. Check customer count. Minimum 3 named customers for a standalone industry page. Fewer than 3: consider grouping under a parent vertical or waiting.
2. Source challenges from sales calls and customer conversations, not desk research. If no sales call data exists, flag the challenges as unvalidated.
3. Map assets to existing `/platform/what-we-monitor/` pages. If the key assets for this industry don't have asset pages yet, build those first.
4. Identify the primary constraint — the specific reason why traditional monitoring fails in this industry. This drives the hero headline and the "Why SAM4" section.
5. Source FAQ objections from actual sales conversations. Draft objections based on competitive intelligence if sales data is unavailable, and flag for validation.
6. Check the `industry-lexicon` skill for this sector. If no lexicon entry exists, build one before writing the page.
7. Follow the 9-section framework from `page-framework.md`. Do not deviate without a documented reason.
