Understanding pump efficiency starts with knowing where you are on the pump curve. But OEM (original equipment manufacturer) pump curves - while useful - don’t always match what’s happening in the field. That mismatch can throw off energy insights and decision-making.

With our latest update, SAM4 now supports calibrated pump curves based on telemetry - giving you a much clearer picture of pump performance and efficiency.
## 📈 Real-world performance, not just specs


SAM4 now lets you:


 	- View actual pump head and flow performance based on historical telemetry

 	- Toggle between OEM and calibrated curves directly in the dashboard

 	- Get more accurate calculations of the Best Efficiency Point (BEP)



This helps operations and energy teams:


 	- Validate how well a pump is performing relative to real-world conditions

 	- Spot pumps that are chronically off-curve and wasting energy

 	- Build stronger business cases for replacement or reconfiguration



“OEM curves are a good starting point - but they’re not the full picture. This update gives our customers a real, data-backed view of pump performance,” said Gianlucca Frasso (Lead Energy Efficiency Analysis at Samotics).

.

![](https://samotics.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Calibrated-pump-curve-vs-OEM-documentation-pump-curve-1-e1744896148663.png) OEM documentation pump curves show what’s expected. Calibrated pump curves show what’s really happening. By comparing the two, SAM4 reveals how pumps actually perform in the field - turning assumptions into actionable insights.
## ⚙️ How it works


When telemetry is available (head & flow), SAM4 now overlays a calibrated curve that reflects observed pump behavior. This allows the system to:


 	- Detect shifts in pump performance over time

 	- Deliver more accurate energy insights (e.g., kWh/m³, BEP deviation)

 	- Improve prioritization of energy-saving recommendations



For customers without complete telemetry, this work lays the foundation for future estimation models using proxy data.
## 🚀 Available now


The calibrated pump curve feature is now live for supported pumps across all customers. It’s already improving the accuracy of energy analyses in major water utilities.
